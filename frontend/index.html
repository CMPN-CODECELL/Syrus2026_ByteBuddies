How It Powers Your Work

Every Feature, Fully Connected

Click any card to jump straight into that part of the studio and try it live.

🧠
AI Image Analysis
Upload any jewelry photo and Claude's vision model reads the actual pixels — it identifies ring vs bracelet vs pendant, counts the gemstones, detects the metal color, and writes a plain-English description of your piece.
📸 Upload a photo →
🔷
Instant 3D Generation
The AI result drives a parametric mesh builder — ring bands, prong clusters, bail loops, tennis stone lines, brooch frames — all constructed in Three.js and rendered in your browser in seconds. No backend, no waiting.
💍 Try a demo ring →
✨
Live Material Swap
Once the 3D model loads, click any metal swatch — Yellow Gold, Platinum, Rose Gold — or any gemstone — Diamond, Ruby, Sapphire — and the model updates instantly. Colors, metalness, roughness and light transmission all change in real time.
🎨 Open Material Studio →
⬇
GLB & STL Export
Export your customized 3D model as a GLB file (for Blender, Unity, Sketchfab, web viewers) or STL (for 3D printing and CAD). All geometry is scaled 10× so 1 unit = 1 mm — ready for production.
⬇ Export a demo model →

Step-by-Step

From Photo to 3D in Four Steps

Every step below is live — click "Try it" and the studio will execute that step for you right now.

01
Upload Your Photo
Drop any jewelry photo into the upload panel. Works with rings, bracelets, earrings, pendants and brooches on any background — studio or table shot.
02
Claude Reads the Image
Claude's vision model analyzes shape, gem count, metal colour, and prong layout. It returns a JSON description that drives the 3D builder. No manual input needed.
03
3D Model Builds Live
A parametric mesh constructs the exact jewelry type — correct prong count, stone placement, bail loop or tennis line — and renders in your browser with full orbit controls.
04
Customise & Export
Click any metal swatch or gemstone to update the model live. When done, export as GLB for Blender or as STL for a 3D printer — geometry is pre-scaled to millimetres.
